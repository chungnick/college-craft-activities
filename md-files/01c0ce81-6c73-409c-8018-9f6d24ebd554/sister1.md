## Housing

Just like a college student, students reside in a high\-rise residence hall in a shared suite with roommates. There are two rooms per suite and suites will have up to 4\-7 students with 1\-2 ensuite bathrooms. Rooms are gender\-segregated and contain an extra\-long twin\-size bed, desks, drawers, and a closet.

Summer Springboard staff will live on your hall to create a community, provide support, plan activities and help you get the most out of your stay in New York. Other members of the Summer Springboard staff also live on campus and are available to support students and tackle emergencies, 24 hours a day.

## Security

All dormitories have 24\-hour security. University security is on 24\-hour patrol and available to any student that may need assistance.

## Facilities

The residence hall is a high\-rise building in Midtown Manhattan, within walking distance to the New York School of Interior Design building, where classes are held. Each suite shares a kitchenette with a fridge, microwave and kitchen table. There are 2 lounges in the building for use during free time, gym access, and laundry access.

Classrooms at NYSID are curated for designers and artists, with the latest technology available. Students will be assigned a homeroom class where they will have a dedicated drafting table and computer. Students will have access to the library, printers and fabric library.

## Dining

Most meals will be catered or eaten at local eateries, conveniently located near the dormitory. We can accommodate diets such as vegetarian, vegan, gluten\-free, kosher, halal, or specific food allergies.
