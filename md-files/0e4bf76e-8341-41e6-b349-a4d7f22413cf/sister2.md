# Compliance Training

## Training Courses

Each course lists the population that should complete the course, the time frame the course should initially be completed, and how frequently the training must be repeated.

All courses can be found online through [Penn State's Learning Resource Network](http://lrn.psu.edu/).

Values, Ethics, and Compliance Training
:   * All full-time and part-time employees, graduate assistants, and graduate fellows.
    * Initial requirement: Within 30 days of beginning employment.
    * Frequency of repetition: Annual, with a new course released each year.

Reporting Suspected Child Abuse: Non-Authorized Adults
:   * All employees, volunteers, independent contractors, graduate assistants, and graduate fellows
    * Initial requirement: Within 30 days of beginning work or volunteer activity
    * Frequency of repetition: None (incorporated into ACT)

Reporting Suspected Child Abuse: Authorized Adults
:   * [Authorized Adults](http://dev.universityethics.psu.edu/resources-and-training/compliance-training-plan#definitions)
    * Initial requirement: Prior to beginning work or volunteer activity with children
    * Frequency of repetition: Annual, due one year from last completion

Clery Act
:   * [Campus Security Authorities (CSAs)](http://dev.universityethics.psu.edu/resources-and-training/compliance-training-plan#definitions)
    * Initial requirement: Within 30 days of beginning employment.
    * Frequency of repetition: None (incorporated into ACT)

HIPAA Privacy Rule Training
:   * All workforce members within a [HIPAA Covered Component](https://universityethics.psu.edu/our-expertise/privacy/hipaa) of the University
    * Initial requirement: within a reasonable period of time after the new staff member begins their employment or activity with the University
    * Frequency of repetition: annually

Understanding Title IX
:   * All employees, graduate assistants, and graduate fellows.
    * Initial requirement: Within 30 days of beginning employment.
    * Frequency of repetition: None (incorporated into Values, Ethics, and Compliance Training)

### Guidance Regarding Non-Compliance

* The Values, Ethics, and Compliance 2025 training will be released on March 24, 2025.
* The due date is April 24, 2025 to be eligible for Annual Salary Increase (ASI).
* Employees who complete the training **after April 24 but by May 15, 2025 at 11:59PM**, will have their ASI award delayed by six months, starting on January 1, 2026 with no retroactive payment.
* Employees who do not complete the training by May 15, 2025, will not be eligible for ASI.
* Individuals will receive five reminder notifications from the LRN to complete the training by April 24, 2025, with their supervisors being copied on the final three reminders.
* **After April 24, 2025**, any employees that have not completed the training will receive an additional four reminders to complete the training by May 15, 2025 to receive delayed ASI, with their supervisors copied on all four reminders.
* Employees that do not complete their training by April 24, 2025 will receive a notification that their ASI award will be delayed by six months, and those that do not complete their training by May 15, 2025 will receive a notification that they will not be eligible for ASI.
* All employees, both full-time and part-time, are required to take this training.
* New employees will also be given 30 days to complete the training.
* Supervisors will be responsible for communicating training requirements for employees without email access. They also must provide time during the work day and computer access for their employees to complete training.
* Unit leaders and supervisors should be held accountable for ensuring their employees complete the training or are given the appropriate consequence. Failure to do one or the other should be taken into account during annual performance reviews.
* HRSPs and HRCs will assist to the extent possible, but will not be held responsible for following-up on non-compliant individuals.
* Individuals on sabbatical or other leave types will be assigned the training upon their return and given 30 days to complete the training.

### Compliance Designations

Campus Security Authority (CSA)
:   * Police or security related positions
    * Individuals to whom crime should be reported
    * Employees with significant responsibilities for student and campus activities
    * [AD74](https://policy.psu.edu/policies/ad74)

CSA Exempt
:   * Licensed professional and pastoral counselors acting in that role
    * [AD74](https://policy.psu.edu/policies/ad74)

Authorized Adult
:   * Individuals who have direct contact with children
    * Individuals with certain specified roles (healthcare provider, library employee, clergy, law enforcement)
    * [AD72](https://policy.psu.edu/policies/ad72)

Title IX Confidential
:   * Licensed professional and pastoral counselors
    * Non-professional counselors or advocates (work or volunteer in health or advocacy center)
    * [AD85](https://policy.psu.edu/policies/ad85)

Title IX Responsible
:   * All Penn State employees who are not Confidential under Title IX
    * [AD85](https://policy.psu.edu/policies/ad85)

Code of Conduct Signee
:   * Student athletes and employees directly involved with any NCAA-sanctioned intercollegiate athletic team at the University Park campus
    * [CA Code of Conduct](https://universityethics.psu.edu/our-expertise/athletics-integrity)
