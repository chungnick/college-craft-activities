# CompSciConnect

Application Deadlines

Applications will open Monday, January 26, 2026.

CompSciConnect is a three-year program that introduces middle school students to programming concepts. Over 120 students participate in CompSciConnect each year.

Over three years, students explore block-based programming like Scratch (Yellow), web design programming with HTML, CSS and JavaScript (Red), and 3D game design programming (Terp). They also learn cybersecurity/safety, cryptology, number bases, logic and computer skills. CompSciConnect emphasizes how each of these skills connects to other areas of study and to making the world a better place.

We recruit completers from our summer middle and high school programs to become teaching assistants and stay engaged with the Computing Catalyst as they move on to college and beyond.

## Frequently Asked Questions

When will CompSciConnect run this year?

In summer 2026, the Computing Catalyst will only host Yellow (rising 6th graders) and Terp (rising 8th graders). CompSciConnect will run in two sessions this year:

Session 1: June 22 - July 2  
Session 2: June 22 - July 2

Who should apply for CompSciConnect?

* All rising 6th graders will be considered for Yellow ONLY.
* All rising 7th graders will be considered for Yellow or Red ONLY.
* All rising 8th graders will be considered for Terp ONLY.

How much does CompSciConnect cost?

The cost to attend this program is $400 for two weeks of camp. There is no additional charge for the meetings during the school year. This fee covers program materials and activity costs. Please do not include the fee when applying. The fee is due upon acceptance to the program.

Who teaches CompSciConnect?

University of Maryland undergraduate students serve as computing instructors by leading campers through the curriculum and supporting them with school-year projects.

Is CompSciConnect a residential program?

No, CompSciConnect is not a residential program.

Can my child join CompSciConnect after 6th grade?

CompSciConnect typically begins with rising 6th graders joining the Yellow group. We usually only accept students into Yellow and Red. CompSciConnect has 45 to 50 spots for Yellow and about 10 to 12 spots for Red.

What is the time commitment for CompSciConnect?

CompSciConnect is a three-year commitment where students meet during the summer for two consecutive weeks and in the school year for one Sunday per month. Attendance is encouraged at the annual CompSciConnect Showcase in December and Maryland Day in April.

When should my child apply?

Students should apply between January and March of each year.

Can male middle-school students apply?

Yes. All students are welcome to apply.

How should home-schooled students apply?

If your child is home-schooled, complete the regular application. Their placement will be based on age and previous computing experience noted in the application.
