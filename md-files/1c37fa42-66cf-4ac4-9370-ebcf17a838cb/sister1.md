## PIPS Application Process

Applications for the Summer 2026 cohort of the PIPS program are open and will close February 23, 2026 at 11:59pm PST.

[Click Here to Apply](https://pips.embark.com/apply/2026)

## PIPS Eligibility Requirements

Students must:

* Currently be a rising junior or senior in high school.
* Be at least 16 years old by the start of the program.

* Reside and attend high school in one of the following counties in Northern California:

  + San Francisco
  + San Mateo
  + Santa Clara
  + Marin
  + Alameda
  + Contra Costa
  + Santa Cruz
* Have a cumulative unweighted high school GPA of at least 3.0 (including Fall 2025 grades).
* Have access to a computer and internet for the duration of the internship.
* Be available to start the program on June 8, 2026 and have no other commitments that interfere with the 30 hour per week commitment of the PIPS program.

## Application Steps

1. Review the components of the application and prepare all materials needed.

2. Prepare the following attachments for the application:

* **Resume**

  + Please upload a resume that summarizes your education, work, and extracurricular activities.
* **Academic Record**
  + We require an unofficial transcript for each school or academic program you have taken courses at while in high school.
  + Your high school transcript must include Fall 2025 grades.
* **Letter of Recommendation**

  + 1 letter of recommendation must be included with your application. This letter must be from a high school science or math teacher.
  + In the application, you will enter your recommender's contact information and they will receive an email from the application system to submit the letter.
* **Income Verification**

  + If you are applying for a need-based stipend, please upload 1 of the following as income verification:

    - First two pages of parent/guardians’ most recent 1040 or 1099 tax return
    - Social Security benefits letter
    - Unemployment benefits letter
    - Proof of eligibility for free or reduced-price lunch
    - Other income verification document if you are unable to provide official documentation

3. Submit your application and materials by the application deadline.

4. Submit payment for the application fee of $50 (non-refundable).

5. Fee waiver option: The application fee can be waived if a student's gross family income is under $90,000 or if the student has special circumstances that make it difficult to pay. We encourage applications from students from low socioeconomic backgrounds and will waive the application fee in these instances.

* Students can apply for a fee waiver after creating a PIPS application account. Then proceed to the "Application Fee" tab to apply for a fee waiver.
* Waiver Request Deadline: All waiver requests must be submitted by Sunday, February 22, 2026. We strongly encourage students to submit waiver applications early to avoid delays in submitting their PIPS application.
