# Summer Science Academy

The Summer Science Academy Day Program is a two-week science enrichment program that offers a series of lectures, labs, and field experiences for high school students. These "hands-on" experiences are designed to heighten students' interest in science, increase awareness of careers in science and health care, and introduce and/or reinforce some principles of science.

**Summer Science Academy registration has been postponed. We will update our website soon with further details by Feb 2.**

Summer Science Academy Day Program offers an exciting and engaging **hands-on** experience for passionate **incoming 9th and 10th grade students** with an interest in sciences: biology, health care, medicine, or related STEM fields. The 2026 Academy will take place in-person on Emory's main campus where you and your Academy mates will learn a variety of topics such as:

* Natural Science
* Biotechnology
* Engineering and Math
* Medical and Science Research
* Health and Science Careers Exploration

## FAQs

**What are the hours of the Academy?**

9:00 a.m. - 3:30 p.m., Monday-Friday

**How many students are in each session?**

20-25

**Do students stay on campus for the duration of the Academy?**

No, Summer Science Academy does not have a residential component. Students must be dropped off and picked up each day.

**Is the Academy open to students in the 9-12 grades?**

Summer Science Academy is only open to students who will be an incoming 9th or 10th grade student. We do not accept incoming 11th or 12th grade students.

**Can I switch my child from one session to another?**

We prefer not to make changes if they can be avoided. However, we understand changes do arise. If space is available, we will make the proper accommodations.

**Are refunds available?**

Yes, refunds are available through May 4, 2026 - $50 cancellation fee applies. If refund desired after May 4, there will be a $200 cancellation fee. No refunds will be issued after the start of the academy session.

**Who will be teaching my student?**

Qualified 1st and 2nd year medical students, health professions students, and/or teachers who have agreed to spend their summer cultivating the next generation of medical and health professionals.

**Are students responsible for their own supplies?**

We supply all of the materials, supplies, and fun for participants!

**What are COVID-19 protocols for participation?**

We strongly encourage that all students be fully vaccinated. Though not a requirement, full vaccination helps reduce the transmission of the virus among fellow students. We strongly suggest that students bring masks with them on excursion days.

**What are the considerations for out-of-state students?**

Out-of-state students must have a guardian available and responsible for pick-up and drop-off, medical decisions in the event of a health concern, and ensure that the student has lunch each day.

**Will students be provided lunch?**

No, students are to bring their personal lunch each day. Lunches should be in a container that can preserve the integrity of the food items until lunch time.
