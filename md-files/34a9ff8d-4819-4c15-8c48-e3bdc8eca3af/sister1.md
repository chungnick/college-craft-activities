## For Students and Families: PTA Reflections

The PTA Reflections program provides access to the arts and opportunities for recognition to help students explore their own thoughts, feelings and ideas using art and to develop confidence and a love for learning that will help them become more successful in school and in life.

* **Inspire**students and families to creatively engage around the annual theme.

* **Share**resources to support families' artistic endeavors and access arts education opportunities.

* **Provide** student artists access to recognition and celebrations for their creative talent.

* **Encourage** continued participation in the arts.

##### 2025-2026 Reflections Theme: *I Belong!*

### **How to Participate**

Express your interest in participating to your local PTA to collect details on participation options, the submission process, deadlines and arts category and division requirements.  
  
Managed at the state level, Reflections participation depends on each state PTA's forms, deadlines, and guidelines. Students must participate through a PTA and must follow the processes specific to the PTA and their state’s program. You cannot submit entries directly to National PTA.

##### **Not sure if your school has a PTA?**

Check out your school's website, ask someone in your school's front office and use [**Find a PTA**](https://member.pta.org/Run-Your-PTA/PTA-Lookup) tool to see what options are available.

[Find a PTA](https://member.pta.org/Run-Your-PTA/PTA-Lookup "Find a PTA")

##### **What if my local PTA does not support the Reflections program?**

Visit your [**state PTA**](https://www.pta.org/home/programs/reflections#guidelines) Reflections website to explore additional participation options that may be available to students without a local PTA.

### Reflections Helpful Hints

In Reflections, students participate through the arts categories and divisions available through their PTA. Be sure your entries follow the arts category and division rules and requirements outlined by your PTA.

##### Divisions

Students participate in the appropriate division for their grade: Primary (Pre-K - Grade 2), Intermediate (Grades 3-5), Middle School (Grades 6-8), High School (Grades 9-12), Accessible Arts (All grades welcome).

The Accessible Arts Division is a participation option for students who identify as having a disability and may receive services under IDEA or ADA: Section 504.

National PTA outlines [frequently asked questions](https://www.pta.org/home/programs/reflections#faq) and offers general answers about the Reflections program. Topics range from general program details, category-specific tips and participation details.

##### Reflections Reminders

Here are high-level reminders for students and families to keep in mind while preparing to participate in Reflections:

* Reflections is a PTA program, and **any student interested in participating in must do so through a [PTA](https://member.pta.org/Run-Your-PTA/Find-a-PTA "Find a PTA")** and in the arts categories and divisions offered.

* Reflections prioritizes the student artist's interpretation of theme and **entries should clearly show a connection to the annual theme**.

* Student artists must **create new, original artwork** in response to the annual theme for each Reflections program.

* Entries must be **the original work of one student only** and only one student may be recognized for each entry on the national level.

### **Reflections Celebrations Awards**

National PTA believes that celebrating student achievement in the arts encourages students to keep creating and growing as artists. Positive recognition also strong family engagement, which result in higher rates of student success in school and in life.

#### Additional Reflections Awards

##### **Join the Annual Reflections Theme Search Contest!**

Each year, through the Annual Reflections Theme Search Contest National PTA selects one student theme each year to inspire a future Reflections program. The national winner receives a $100 award and national recognition. Themes are chosen two years in advance.

Students can participate through a PTA and by following their state PTA's process and guidelines.   
  
For details on how to join, contact your [state PTA](https://www.pta.org/home/programs/reflections#guidelines "State Reflections Program") or **[visit the Reflections Awards Page](https://www.pta.org/home/programs/reflections/awards#:~:text=in%20Spanish.-,Annual%20Theme%20Search%20Contest,-Congratulations%202024%20Reflections "Theme Search Contest")**.
