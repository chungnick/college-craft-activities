# Tuition & Financing

## Overview

*Tuition and fees are subject to Board of Trustees approval and may change.*

### 2026 Tuition and Fees

Summer 2026 tuition and fees will be posted in late Spring

#### 2025 Summer Session Courses Per Point

Summer Session Courses: $2,574 Per Point (Credit)

**2025 Tuition Calculation Example**  
Student enrolled in 6 points (typically equivalent to 2 courses)  
6 points × $2,574 per point = $15,444

### 2025 Special Course-Specific Rates

**Special Foreign Language Programs**

* Arabic Language Courses: $6,564 per course
* Hindi-Urdu Language Courses: $4,376 per course
* Russian Language Courses: $6,564 per course

**0-Credit Courses**  
The following 0-point courses will be charged at the 2-point rate:

* Chemistry S0001: PREPARATION-COLLEGE CHEMISTRY
* Physics S0065: BASIC PHYSICS
* MATH S0065: BASIC MATHEMATICS

*\*Please note discussion or recitation sections carry no additional cost*

Payment Dates & Procedures

**Billing**

Columbia University bills students for tuition and fees according to the schedule below. When a statement is generated, an email notification is sent directly to your Columbia email account. You can also access a copy of the bill through [Student Services Online (SSOL)](https://ssol.columbia.edu/). *The University does not mail paper bills.*

After the first statement is generated in June, bills will be generated periodically if you make schedule changes or if you carry an account balance.

**Payment Instructions**

For payment instructions and to authorize a parent to pay your bill, see [Student Financial Services](https://sfs.columbia.edu/content/how-do-i-pay).

**Important Billing and Payment Dates**

Please visit Student Financial Services' [Billing Schedule](https://www.sfs.columbia.edu/bill-schedule) for the most up-to-date dates.

## Financial Resources

We want to make sure that the cost of your studies does not stand in the way of your joining us this summer and accomplishing your goals.

We are providing you with information about funding opportunities at Columbia this summer and other outside funding sources to consider.

### Visiting Student Merit Fellowship

Visiting Students who are enrolled in a summer visiting program or a summer CPA program, and are enrolled in two or more courses, can apply for a merit-based fellowship. Accepted students will receive up to $5,000 to cover course tuition. Please visit the [Visiting Student Merit Fellowship webpage](https://summer.sps.columbia.edu/visiting-student-admissions/tuition-financing/visiting-student-merit-fellowship) to learn more.

### Outside Funding Sources

You can find a list of scholarship search sites and outside award processing details [here](https://sfs.columbia.edu/content/scholarships). 

**Private Loans**

For information on alternative education loans, please visit [Student Financial Services’ Graduate Private Loans webpage](https://sfs.columbia.edu/financial-aid/private-loans?_gl=1*15y1l2b*_gcl_au*MTU0MDQ5OTgyMi4xNzI4NDAyNTcw)

**U.S. Military Veterans**

The School of Professional Studies participates in [the Yellow Ribbon Program](https://sfs.columbia.edu/content/yellow-ribbon-program-0), an initiative authorized by the Veterans Educational Assistance Act of 2008 (the Post-9/11 GI Bill®\*) in which educational institutions provide eligible student veterans with a tuition waiver or grant matched by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. This program supplements the base educational benefits provided by the Post-9/11 GI Bill®.

The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, as well as state and local government, offers additional educational assistance programs for veterans of the U.S. Armed Forces and their dependents.
