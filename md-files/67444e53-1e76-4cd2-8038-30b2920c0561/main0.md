## An engaging cybersecurity summer camp in the heart of downtown Denver

# Summer Camp for High School Students

The GenCyber Summer Camp program at the CU Denver Business School provides exciting, hands-on cybersecurity experiences for students at the K-12 level. Students will explore various aspects of cybersecurity with hands-on exercises and games, including ethical hacking, social engineering, computer and digital forensics, networking fundamentals, cyber threats, cybersecurity careers, ethics in cybersecurity, and online safety.

No previous cybersecurity knowledge is required. **CU Denver GenCyber is entirely free** and open to **all high school students in Colorado**. Camp materials, including Raspberry Pi kits, virtual labs, and games, will be provided free of charge for hands-on learning. Students will receive a Certificate of Achievement at the end of the camp. Funding is jointly provided by the National Security Agency and National Science Foundation.

**When:** July 15-19, 2024; 9:00 am - 4:00 pm (Online)

# **Summer Camp Program Overview**

#### Day 1: Introduction to Cybersecurity

- Demonstrate knowledge of cybersecurity concepts and principles  
- Differentiate among cybersecurity, information security, and information privacy. Understand why they are important.  
- Recognize the risks of online activities and countermeasures to protect personal data and identity  
- Describes stages of cyber intrusions (reconnaissance, delivery, exploitation  
- Describe different types of cybercrime and attackers  
- Explain attack categories (interception, interruption, modification, etc.)

#### Day 2: Cybersecurity Ethics and Career Paths

- Define and describe ethics in relations to security and privacy based on Daniels Fund Initiative framework  
- Explain ethical behavior in the context of cybersecurity.  
- Give an example of an online ethical behavior  
- Understand concepts, such as cyberbullying, netiquette, Internet trolls  
- Recognize the importance of online safety in different domains: Social media safety, mobile safety, gaming safety, etc.  
- Learn about ethical hacking and penetration testing.  
- Distinguish between different types of cybersecurity careers

#### Day 3: Behavioral Security

- Social engineering and footprinting.
  
- Identify different types of social engineering attacks: Ransomware, scareware, phishing, pretexting, baiting, fishing, etc.  
- Explain the reasons why people fall victim to social engineering: Human vulnerabilities and cognitive biases  
- Illustrate good and bad practices on social media: Think before you post, text, or share, or accept.  
- Understand the importance of Personally identifiable information (PII).  
- Gather PII online through active and passive footprinting. Learn how to hide PII during online activities.

#### Day 4: Technical Security

- Computer systems and networking concepts.   
- Understand TCP/IP protocol stack, routing on Internet and devices.  
- Understand important classes of attacks: DDoS, APT, Malwares.  
- Understand role of security technologies: firewalls, IDS/IPS, VPN.  
- Understand the importance and components of cryptography: digital certificates, PKI, encryption algorithms.  
- Configure your RPi and Kali Linux. Create fully functional “throw-away hacking” computer.  
- Learn how to use your hacking computer for penetration testing.

#### Day 5: Computer and Digital Forensics

- Understand the differences between computer and digital forensics  
- Perform forensic investigation  
- Media, audio, image, network forensics  
- How to hide information in digital media.  
- The Internet of Things (IoT)  (e.g. wearable devices, smart assistants). Recognize how hackers and/or companies can misuse data acquired through such devices.  
- Learn basic data analysis techniques to interpret datasets that contain personal data.
