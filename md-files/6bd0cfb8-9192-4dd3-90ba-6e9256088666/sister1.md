R-STEM and partner organizations at Rice University offer a wide range of summer enrichment opportunities for elementary, middle, and high school students—including computer science, engineering, biotech, digital health, energy, design, and hands-on STEAM exploration.

**Before You Register:**  
Please note that each R-STEM Summer Student Program has its own “Things to Know Before Registering” section. Requirements, important details, and program-specific instructions are provided directly on each program’s page. We strongly encourage families to review these details carefully before beginning the registration or application process.

### **Elementary School**

#### **STEM-Letics Academy**

A joint effort with [Rice Athletics](https://riceowls.com/), STEM-Letics Academy is week-long academy offered multiple weeks during the summer for current 3rd, 4th, and 5th grade students (2025-2026 current school year). This academy is designed to keep students both mentally and physically active during the summer by incorporating STEM and physical activities.

**[LEARN MORE](https://research.rice.edu/rstem/stem-letics) >**

### 

### **Elementary & Middle School**

#### **STEAMotion**

STEAMotion is a dynamic one-week summer academy for grades K-8 (2025-2026 current school year) crafted by the Rice Office of STEM Engagement in collaboration with various education agencies containing STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics) activities.

**[LEARN MORE](https://research.rice.edu/rstem/steamotion) >**

### **Elementary/Middle/High School**

#### **STEAM Academies**

The Rice STEAM Academies is an immersive, hands-on summer experience for students in grades 3–9. The program is offered in two engaging sections: STEAM-Up and Spark Academy, giving students the opportunity to explore creativity, innovation, and real-world problem-solving through dynamic, challenge-based learning in science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics.

**[LEARN MORE](https://research.rice.edu/rstem/steam-academies) >**

### **Middle School**

#### **Rice Computer Engineering Design Academy**

Rice Computer Engineering Design Academy is a week-long program for current 6th, 7th, and 8th graders (2025-2026 current school year) to learn about Arduino, Computer-Aided Design (CAD) programming, and rapid prototyping.

**[LEARN MORE](https://research.rice.edu/rstem/ced) >**

### **Middle & High School**

#### **Forensics Investigators Program**

This one-week student program for students in Grades 7-10 (2025-2026 current school year) aims to introduce the science behind crime scenes, covering topics such as evidence collection and classification, blood evidence, toxicology, DNA, and more.

**[LEARN MORE](https://research.rice.edu/rstem/forensics-investigators-program) >**

#### **Rice Bio Academies**

The Rice Bio Academies is an immersive, hands-on summer experience for high school students currently in 9th through 11th grade. The program is offered in two focused sections: Biotech and Biomed, giving students the opportunity to explore key areas of science through real-world applications and cutting-edge lab techniques.

**[LEARN MORE](https://research.rice.edu/rstem/dcc) >**

### **Workshops**

#### **R-STEM Academy**

R-STEM Academy is a series of one-day workshops held annually in the spring at Rice University for current 2nd-12th grade students to participate in STEM lessons designed by experienced teachers inspired by the ongoing research at Rice University.

**[LEARN MORE](/rstem/nanoacademy) >**

#### **Rice Wireless Innovators Lab**

The Rice Wireless Innovators Lab offers a one-day, hands-on STEM workshop for high school students focused on wireless communication, artificial intelligence (AI), and machine learning.

### 

**[LEARN MORE](https://research.rice.edu/rstem/rice-wireless-innovators-lab) >**

#### **Young Scholars Invent Workshop**

Young Scholars Invent is a 1.5-day program where students tackle real-world challenges with cross-school teammates, engage in active design, build and test their solutions, and present to a panel of judges. The focus is on collaborative problem-solving, mirroring industry environments.

**[LEARN MORE](https://research.rice.edu/rstem/young-scholars-invent) >**

### **Sunset Programs**

#### **Energy and Environmental Engineering Academy**

Energy and Environmental Engineering Academy was a week-long program for 8th–10th grade students designed to explore the future of energy and sustainability. Through hands-on experiments, workshops, and projects, students investigated fossil fuels, renewables, and emerging technologies.

#### **Houston Environmental Action Learning**

The Houston Environmental Action Learning (HEAL) program was a free two-week summer experience designed to engage 9th and 10th grade students in environmental participatory action research and increase awareness of environmental challenges and issues.

#### **Introduction to Research and Innovative Design in Engineering (iRIDE)**

iRIDE was a hands-on program designed to introduce middle school students in specific Houston-area communities to the challenging and diverse field of engineering.

#### **Data Divas**

Data Divas empowered female students in grades 11–12 with a week-long immersion into the world of data science, blending comprehensive learning with hands-on activities. Guided by successful female professionals, participants developed essential skills, culminating in a collaborative team presentation to showcase their newfound expertise.
