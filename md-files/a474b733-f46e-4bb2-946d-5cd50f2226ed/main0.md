# CURE Research Internship Program

The CURE Research Internship is an opportunity for high school and City College of San Francisco students interested in health careers to spend 8-weeks at UCSF supporting various types of research with the primary objectives of:

1. Immersing promising students in career opportunities across the spectrum of research, including basic, clinical, behavioral, and population sciences;
2. Providing positive training and support for participating students so they will declare a major aligned with biomedical research and seriously consider research as a career option.

The CURE program provides students with an exceptional scientific, health, and medical environment to further their career interests, as well as college preparation and academic and professional development.

---

### What Students Will Gain from the Program

---

* CURE Interns will be mentored by UCSF faculty, post-docs, and graduate students.
* CURE Interns will have the opportunity to conduct scientific research and present their research project and findings at our Research Symposium.
* CURE Interns will receive college and career advising and college application support.

---

### What are the Eligibility Requirements

---

* Must be a current 11th grader in high school OR a current community college student at CCSF
* Must be at least 16 years old by the start of the program
* Must have a minimum GPA of 2.5
* Must have completed Biology or Chemistry with a grade of B or better
* Must be able to commit 40 hours/week for 8 weeks

---

### How to Apply

---

##### Online Application & Recommendation

1. Submit the [online student application](https://ucsf.co1.qualtrics.com/jfe/form/SV_08MUIPtImpM3X5s). (You will need to upload your unofficial transcript)
2. One recommendation form is required. Your recommender should fill out and submit the [online recommendation form](https://ucsf.co1.qualtrics.com/jfe/form/SV_2bKq3zHHP0MlDue).

##### Important Program Dates

* Application opens on: Sunday, October 12, 2025
* Application Deadline: Sunday, February 8, 2026, at 11:59PM PST
* Mandatory Program dates:
  + Parent Orientation: Thursday, May 21, 2026
  + Student Orientation: Thursday, June 11. 2026
  + Program duration: Monday, June 15 - Friday, August 7, 2026
