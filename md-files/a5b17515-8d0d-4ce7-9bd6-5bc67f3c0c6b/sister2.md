# FAQs

[Updated: 09-18-23]

Below find answers to common questions about the Tim Draper High School Utah Entrepreneur Challenge (HSUEC). Make sure to also review the competition guide for answers and instructions for how to participate. The guide has important information about eligibility, judging, and other information.

### [Why should I enter the challenge?](#collapse1)

The Tim Draper High School Utah Entrepreneur Challenge is your opportunity to showcase your innovation and entrepreneurial spirit, and win cash and scholarship prizes.

### [Who can compete in the challenge?](#collapse2)

Anyone who is in highschool or ages 14-18 can participate in the challenge.

### [Is there a limit on the size of teams that enter?](#collapse3)

There is no minimum for number of students on a team, but teams may not consist of more than 5 students.

### [How are entries judged?](#collapse3b)

Entries will be judged on the four business components (problem, solution, market, and prototype) as well as their presentation and ability to address the questions asked.

### [Can a team submit more than one idea?](#collapse4)

Yes! There is no limit to how many ideas a single team can submit. Just make sure that each idea is entered as a separate submission.

### [Do we have to have made a real product?](#collapse5)

No, but you should have made a prototype that clearly demonstrates how the product or idea would help solve the identified problem. A prototype can be (but isn’t limited to) an app, website, technology, machinery, cardboard example, or a sketch.

### [Can I use an existing idea/product of mine? Or do I have to create a new idea?](#collapse6)

You and your team may use an idea you have already been working on. There is no need to come up with a completely new idea for this competition. It is suggested you continue to work on your already awesome idea!

### [Are ideas still owned by students once submitted?](#collapse7)

Yes! Neither the Tim Draper High School Utah Entrepreneur Challenge nor the Lassonde Institute takes any ownership in an idea or plan as part of the Tim Draper High School Utah Entrepreneur Challenge.

### [Are my friends or family allowed to watch me present?](#collapse8)

No, only the presenting team members are allowed in the presentation room. Parents and other family members are welcome to come to the public showcase at Lassonde Studios after the presentations.

### [How many teams are chosen as finalists?](#collapse11)

The judges will pick 20 finalists to be invited to the final event.
