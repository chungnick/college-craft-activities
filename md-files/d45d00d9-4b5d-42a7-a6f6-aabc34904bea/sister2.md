    - [Commenting Policy](https://globalyouth.wharton.upenn.edu/commenting-policy/)
      * The Wharton Global Youth Program online community encourages open dialogue from our followers, readers and learners, through commenting on our Global Youth website articles and podcast transcripts and on our various social media platforms, including LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, Twitter and YouTube.
          
           
        While we value all opinions and viewpoints, we also reserve the right to monitor, censor and unpublish posted comments throughout the Global Youth online community if they do not align with the purpose of our platform as a safe, educational space for pre-college learners, or they violate our code of ethics and conduct.

      * The Wharton Global Youth Program embraces generative artificial intelligence (AI) technology and sees it as an important tool for students. However, we recognize that the material generated by these tools may produce inaccurate, incomplete, or otherwise problematic content.
          
           
        High school students applying to and participating in Wharton Global Youth programs, courses and competitions are strongly encouraged to review the Wharton Global Youth Program Statement of AI Use, application integrity statement and accompanying AI policies before submitting any written essays and/or assignments.

## Learn about Costs & Aid Opportunities for Wharton Global Youth Programs

Wharton Global Youth programs provide a comprehensive and transformative educational experience for intellectually-curious high school students. Guided by Wharton’s culture of innovation, hands-on learning, collaboration, impact, and academic excellence, programs are designed to educate and inspire the next generation of global leaders. Fees and additional costs associated with attending programs vary, as outlined below.

## Scholarship Opportunities

The Wharton Global Youth Program is committed to supporting academically driven students with demonstrated financial need. Through a range of need-based scholarships and financial awards, we are proud to offer a limited number of full and partial funding opportunities to eligible program participants. Review the eligibility criteria for the various scholarships outlined below.

*Applicants to the Management and Technology Summer Institute should visit the [M&TSI site](https://fisher.wharton.upenn.edu/management-technology-summer-institute/) for details regarding available program scholarships.*

## Available Scholarships

#### Philadelphia Public School District Scholarships

*Available for all summer on-campus and location-based programs, and non-credit, online programs.*

Students attending a School District of Philadelphia public or charter high school with financial need may be eligible for a full scholarship to attend a Wharton Global Youth summer program. Scholarship funding is limited; we cannot guarantee all applicants will ultimately receive financial assistance. Scholarships are applied toward relevant program fees; there may be additional costs of attending programming, such as transportation, course materials, additional meals, incidentals, etc. Scholarships are awarded to students with a record of academic excellence and demonstrated financial need. Need is determined by a variety of factors including family income, family size, relevant expenses, and additional financial circumstances.

**Eligibility Criteria:**

In order to qualify for a Philadelphia School District Scholarship, students must:

* Be currently enrolled in a School District of Philadelphia public or charter high school
* Be a resident of the City of Philadelphia
* Demonstrate financial need

**Scholarship Application Process:**

* Students interested in applying for a scholarship must indicate this by checking the **Financial Aid box** within their summer program application. After submitting the application, they will receive an email with a link to the Wharton Global Youth Scholarship Application.
* To be considered for aid, your program application must be submitted by the priority deadline of January 28, 2026, at 11:59 PM ET, and your scholarship application must be submitted by February 4, 2026, at 11:59 PM ET.

#### National Education Opportunity Network (NEON) Scholarships

*Available for all summer on-campus and location-based programs, and non-credit, online programs. Excludes the Wharton Pre-baccalaureate Program.*

Students with demonstrated financial need who are currently enrolled in, or have previously completed, a college-credit course through the National Education Opportunity Network (formerly the National Education Equity Lab) may be eligible for a scholarship to attend a Wharton Global Youth summer program. Preference is given to students who have completed the Wharton Personal Finance course, FNCE 0002, in conjunction with NEON. Scholarship funding is limited; we cannot guarantee all applicants will ultimately receive financial assistance. Scholarships are applied toward relevant program fees; there may be additional costs of attending programming, such as transportation, course materials, additional meals, incidentals, etc. Scholarships are awarded to students with a record of academic excellence and demonstrated financial need. Need is determined by a variety of factors including family income, family size, relevant expenses, and additional financial circumstances.

**Eligibility Criteria:**

In order to qualify for a National Education Opportunity Network (NEON) scholarship, students must:

* Be currently enrolled in, or have previously completed, a college-credit course through NEON National Education Equity Lab
* Maintain, or have achieved, strong academic standing in their college course(s)
* Demonstrate financial need

**Scholarship Application Process:**

* Eligible NEON students interested in applying for financial aid or scholarships must contact the Global Youth Office at [whartonyouth@wharton.upenn.edu](mailto:whartonyouth@wharton.upenn.edu) to request a scholarship application. Once their affiliation with NEON is confirmed, students will be emailed a link to complete a scholarship application.
* To be considered for aid, your program application must be submitted by the priority deadline of January 28, 2026, at 11:59 PM ET, and your scholarship application must be submitted by February 4, 2026, at 11:59 PM ET.

## Scholarship Details

*Scholarship information is reviewed confidentially by the Wharton Global Youth Review Committee and does not influence admission decisions.*

* Admission decisions are **need-blind**. Financial need does not impact whether a student is admitted into a program.
* Unfortunately, we are **unable to offer financial aid to all students who apply**. Funding is limited and awarded based on a combination of demonstrated need and academic merit.
* If applying to multiple summer programs, you will only submit **one** scholarship application.
* You do **not** need to select a specific scholarship opportunity; the Wharton Global Youth Review Committee will determine which is the best fit.
* Financial documentation is **not required** for the initial scholarship application, though we reserve the right to request verification.
* Only students who are **admitted into a program** will be considered for scholarship funding.
* Scholarship decisions will be shared within **48 hours of admission notification**.

## Scholarship FAQs

#### Can international students apply for scholarships? Will it impact admissions decisions?

Yes, international students who meet the eligibility criteria of any of the scholarship awards above are more than welcome to apply. Scholarship applications are reviewed independently of program applications and will not impact admissions decisions.

#### Should I Submit More Than One Scholarship Application?

No. The scholarship application does not vary by program, so there is no need to submit more than one scholarship application. If you re applying to multiple summer programs, you need only check off all programs to which you are applying within the scholarship application to be considered.

#### When will I receive my scholarship decision?

Scholarship decisions will be emailed to applicants within 48 hours of the release of their admission decision. Students who are not admitted into a program will be considered ineligible for a program scholarship and will not receive a scholarship notification.

#### What costs does the scholarship cover? How is the award applied?

Program scholarships are applied towards relevant program fees, which vary by program type. Refer to the Program Fees chart above to determine what costs are covered by program fees.

If the scholarship award is full, all program fees will be waived for the program participant. If the scholarship award is partial, the participant will be financially responsible for the balance of remaining program fees.

Any additional costs associated with attending programs, such as textbooks or class materials, transportation to/from campus, additional meals, incidentals, spending money, etc., will be the financial responsibility of the program participant and are not covered by a program scholarship.

#### How do I formally accept or decline the scholarship?

Scholarship decisions will be emailed to students and will include relevant instructions for formally accepting or declining the award. If scholarship recipients do not accept the award within the allotted time frame, they will forfeit the award.

#### Are scholarships transferrable between programs?

As scholarship funding varies by program, unfortunately financial awards may not be transferred between programs.
