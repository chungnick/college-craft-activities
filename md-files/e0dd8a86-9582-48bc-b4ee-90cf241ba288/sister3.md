# Student Scholarships

Apply for your share of $200,000 in scholarships from DECA's corporate partners.

DECA’s scholarship program provides over $200,000 in scholarships to high school and college DECA members at the International Career Development Conference (ICDC) each year. DECA Inc. administers the program based on guidelines set by the donor. DECA scholarships are strictly merit-based.

DECA members will apply for scholarships through [**this website**](https://my.reviewr.com/s2/site/deca_scholarship2025?redir=micrositedesign). Student members will need to create an account on the page. Click "CREATE AN ACCOUNT" and fill in your information to begin your application.  
  
**Applications are open now through January 9, 2026.**

###### How To Apply

**‍**Apply through [**this website**](https://my.reviewr.com/s2/site/deca_scholarship2025?redir=micrositedesign). Members will need to create an account on the page. Click "CREATE AN ACCOUNT" and fill in your information to begin your application.

‍

Applicants will be required to upload a transcript and get three letters of recommendation in addition to any specific requirements for each scholarship.

‍

The 2025-2026 scholarship window will not have a preliminary round, and all required items must be submitted by **January 9, 2026**.

‍

**Applications are open now through January 9, 2026.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

[Are DECA scholarships based on financial need?](#)

No, most of DECA scholarships are merit-based.

[Are unofficial transcripts accepted?](#)

Yes, you may upload unofficial transcripts.

[Can I apply for more than one scholarship?](#)

Yes, you may apply for more than one scholarship providing you meet the criteria of each scholarship.

[Can I use the scholarship if I am a part-time student?](#)

No, you must be enrolled as a full-time student at a college/university.

[Can my scholarship be deferred?](#)

Yes, your scholarship can be deferred up to three (3) years.

[Do I have to attend the International Career Development Conference (ICDC) if I win a scholarship?](#)

No, you do not need to attend DECA ICDC in order to receive your scholarship.

[Do I have to be a current DECA member to apply for DECA scholarships and what are the requirements to become a DECA member?](#)

Yes, you must be a current DECA member to be eligible for DECA scholarships. Ask your school counselor if your school has a DECA chapter and register through the chapter advisor.

[Do I have to be a high school DECA senior to apply?](#)

No, all grade levels (from high school freshmen to Collegiate DECA members) are welcome to apply. Unless the scholarship application specifically requires a grade level/collegiate, you can assume that all members can apply.

[Do I have to be an employee or child of the corporate sponsored scholarships?](#)

You are not required to be a relative/employee to apply for most of our corporate scholarships. Some of our donors give special attention to applicants who work for them. Publix Super Markets does require you to be an associate with their company.

[Do I have to be interested in a particular career or industry for some scholarships?](#)

Some scholarships are focused on a specific industry and/or career interest (hospitality, retail, restaurant management, etc.) and/or School-based Enterprise.

[How will I know if I win a scholarship?](#)

DECA will notify your chapter advisor prior and send you an email to the address you provided in your scholarship profile.

[If I win a scholarship, do I get to meet the sponsor and/or be able to send them a thank you letter?](#)

Yes, you will be able to meet the sponsor if you attend DECA ICDC. DECA will also email you contact information to send a thank you letter.

[Once my application is submitted can I go back in and make changes?](#)

No, once you submit your application you cannot make any further changes.

[What if I have additional questions?](#)

Please email scholarships@deca.org for assistance.

[What is a community leader?](#)

A community leader is an individual that represents the community. Some examples of a community leader: elected officials, local youth councils, camp counselors, coaches, school officials, religious leaders, fire chiefs, supervisor of where you may do/have done community service activity(ies).

[What is a statement of interest?](#)

A statement of interest is an opportunity to share some valuable information about who you are, and why do you feel that you should receive a DECA scholarship. This part of your application gives the review committee to see more than your GPA, by including your future goals and interest. It basically gives you an opportunity to stand out from other applicants and tell your story. There is no word limit to your statement of interest.

[Where are the scholarship monies sent?](#)

Monies are sent directly to the college/university that you will be attending by completing a form that will be emailed to you in May.
