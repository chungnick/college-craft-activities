## Outreach

## FBRI Outreach Mission

To provide and promote technology validation and partnerships that will meet societal needs for fuels, chemicals, and advanced materials in an economically and ecologically sustainable manner.

**Engage Industry & Community**

* Ongoing tours to interested industry and community leaders.
* Leadership Maine (business and non-profit leaders) class and their new Education Leaders (K-12 school administrators) class to learn more about our role in Maine’s economic development, workforce development, sponsored by The Maine Development Foundation and UMaine Foster Center for Innovation.
* Margaret Chase Smith Center’s Maine Leadership Institute.
* Bangor Chamber of Commerce Building Bridges Program for business education Partnership [Building Bridges](https://www.bangorregion.com/affiliated-programs/building-bridges/).

**Engage Government**

Maine Legislators visit to FBRI labs twice every year.

**Engage Students**

* Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU)
* [Self-Made Engineering Leaders of the Future (SELF) for high school girls](https://forestbioproducts.umaine.edu/self/)
* High School/middle school tours year around.
* Girl Scouts tours and hands on activities.
* Expanding your Horizons.
* 4-H members’ tours and hands on activities.
