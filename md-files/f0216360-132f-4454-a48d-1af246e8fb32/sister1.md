# Undergraduate and Pre-College Programs

Winship Cancer Institute of Emory University offers educational and training programs for college undergraduates, middle and high school students, providing them with skills and experiences for careers in cancer research.

## Undergraduate Programs

Winship Cancer Institute provides college undergraduates with educational and training experiences to prepare them for their first steps towards a career in cancer research.

## Summer Opportunity in Cancer Research

The Winship Summer Opportunity in Cancer Research Internship provides a cancer-related summer research experience to college undergraduate students who come from a racial or ethnic background under-represented in STEM.

* [Summer Opportunity in Cancer Research Internship](summer-opportunity-in-cancer-research.php)

## Pre-College Programs

Winship Cancer Institute of Emory University offers learning experiences for middle and high school students that develop academic and professional skills in different areas of cancer-related research.

### [Summer Scholars Research Program](summer-scholars.php)

The Winship Summer Scholars Research Program is a non-paid internship offering high school seniors a unique immersion experience with exposure to real-world cancer research under the guidance of faculty mentors.

### [Data Detectives](https://www.ga-science.org/datadetectives)

[Georgia Science Education Initiative (GSEI)](https://www.ga-science.org/ "Georgia Science Education Initiative") presents Data Detectives, an informal STEM program run as an after-school program for middle school girls across Georgia, and also as a summer academy hosted at Emory University.
